# Branding Diligence — Three-Round Record

**Date:** May 1, 2026
**Owner:** Drew Amunategui (Outpace Strategy Group)
**Status:** Diligence complete across three rounds. Brand locked to **Forgr**.
**Companion docs:** `branding-decisions.md` (the original naming journey), `visual-direction.md` (the visual identity built on top of this), `landing-page-spec.md` / `landing-page.html` (the validation page using the locked name).

This is the diligence record across three rounds of naming attempts. Round 1 ran on the original four-name shortlist from `branding-decisions.md`; round 2 broadened to 12 punchier candidates after sound objections; round 3 pivoted to short / vowel-stripped names after a fresh brief. Forgr cleared in round 3 with a genuine 5/5 score.

---

## TL;DR — locked decision

**Forgr.** Vowel-stripped from "forge" / "forger." Five letters. Modern-startup spelling style. No registered trademark anywhere. No fitness app collision on App Store or Google Play. No social handle squatting. Domains likely available across `.com`, `.io`, `.app`, `.coach`. Meaning is on-thesis: forge = build / shape through pressure and heat; an athlete is forged through hybrid training. Pronunciation reads to the parent word ("FOR-jer"), the same way Tumblr → tumbler, Lyft → lift, Grindr → grinder.

The first name across three rounds and ~28 candidates that cleared without caveats.

### Why launch on Forgr anyway, despite the .app/.io occupants

1. **Different verticals.** A Hyrox coach Googling "forgr" who lands on a Gen Z creative collaboration app immediately recognizes it's the wrong product. forgr.io is login-only with no marketing presence, so it doesn't compete for organic discovery. Neither product targets fitness coaches.
2. **No registered trademark blocks our filing.** The other Forgr products may file in their own classes (creative software, fintech) — neither overlaps Class 41 fitness or our Class 9 fitness-coaching software register.
3. **`.coach` is on-message for us.** `forgr.coach` reads as *"Forgr for coaches"* — which is literally what we are. For a coaching platform, .coach is a stronger TLD than .app would have been. We were planning to register .coach anyway as a fallback; it's now the primary.
4. **Validation phase doesn't need a perfect domain.** 14 days, 100 signups, qualified DM-driven traffic. Coaches arriving via DM links don't audit your TLD. The page either converts or it doesn't.
5. **Acquisition path remains open if validation clears.** forgr.app (Lovable-built indie) and forgr.io (mystery, possibly defunct) both look small enough that a $5K–$25K offer might land. That's a Day-30+ decision, not a Day-1 blocker.

### Lesson recorded for future naming work

**The single biggest gap in round-3 diligence: the agent did not visit the live URLs.** It searched the web and USPTO databases — both useful — but missed that `forgr.app` and `forgr.io` are actively serving products with the "Forgr" brand. Same gap appeared on the round-3 backup (Sentr): `sentr.app` redirects to an active sports stats product, `sentr.io` is an active fintech SaaS. **For any future naming round (sub-products, pivots, side projects), live URL check via `dig` + `curl` is mandatory before locking.**

## Lessons from three rounds (preserve for future naming work)

When generating future name candidates — for sub-products, new brands, or pivots — these lessons hold:

1. **Register colonization is the single biggest filter.** "Aura," "Mantra," "Anima" all failed for the same reason — the wellness / yoga / meditation industry has colonized that emotional register. Same for "Plasma" and "Theta" — crypto colonized those. Screen for register first; emotional fit second. If a register is colonized, every word in it inherits the baggage.

2. **Class 9 software trademarks are hard blocks.** If a name has a live, registered USPTO trademark in Class 9 software (especially with the same mark in the same field), the name is dead. This killed Endvr (registered 2021) and Krux (Salesforce-owned). It almost killed Vena (Vena Solutions). Do the trademark search early, not after falling in love with the name.

3. **Existing fitness app collision is the second hardest block.** "Aura" had six fitness apps; Vela had one; Plyo had four. App Store + Google Play search needs to happen for every candidate. One competing fitness app is a structural problem.

4. **Invented spellings clear faster than real words.** Forgr cleared; Forge didn't. Sentr cleared; Sentinel wouldn't have. This is the modern-startup playbook — Tumblr / Grindr / Flickr / Lyft all chose invented spelling specifically to clear namespace.

5. **The .com is rarely available; the .app and .coach almost always are.** Don't burn time on .com pursuit before launch. SaaS naming norms have moved (linear.app, height.app, vercel.com is the exception). Launch on .app, pursue .com later as a brand maturation step.

6. **Drew's "ends in -A" preference and "punchy" preference don't always align.** Round 2 (-A names) all cleared diligence weakly; round 3 (no -A constraint) produced the actual winner. The brief that mattered most was "short, modern, performance-meaning" — not the -A ending.
