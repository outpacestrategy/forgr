# Hyrox Coach Outreach — DM Script & Sequence

**Channel:** Instagram DM primary. LinkedIn as fallback if you find them there and IG is dead.
**Cadence:** Day 1 → Day 3 → Day 7. After Day 7, stop. Move to "passed" in the DB. Do not stack more follow-ups.
**Tone:** Founder-doing-research, not salesperson. You don't have an app. You have a question. Curious, specific, warm, low-stakes.
**Goal of every message:** Book a 20-minute call. Not a pitch. Not a survey link. A call.

---

## Pre-send checklist (do this every time)

- [ ] Read their last 3 posts. Pick one specific thing to reference (a race result, a coaching post, a client win, a programming take).
- [ ] Confirm they actually coach (bio says coach + has an offer link / "DM to apply" / FITR / TrueCoach / Trainerize / website).
- [ ] Confirm they're not on a vacation/injury arc — don't open a research convo with someone who just posted about a death or a setback.
- [ ] Use their first name. Never "Hey coach" or "Hi there."
- [ ] Don't paste — type fresh. IG flags identical messages fast.

---

## Day 1 — Cold open (the only one that really matters)

> Hey {first name} — saw your {specific reference, e.g. "post breaking down the run-ski transition" / "Miami race recap" / "client's sub-90 PR"}. Really good.
>
> Quick context: I'm building something for Hyrox coaches and I'm in the research phase, not selling anything. Trying to figure out how coaches who actually do this for a living run their day-to-day — programming, client comms, billing, all of it.
>
> Would you be open to a 20-min call? Happy to send a $25 coffee gift card for your time. No pitch, just questions.
>
> Either way, appreciate the work you put out.

**Why this works:**
- Specific reference proves you're not blasting.
- "Research phase, not selling" lowers their guard immediately.
- "How coaches who do this for a living run their day-to-day" implicitly flatters — you're treating them as an expert.
- Gift card makes it feel like a real ask, not a freebie hunt.
- "Either way…" ends warm even if they ignore.

**Length target:** 4-6 lines. If it scrolls, cut it.

---

## Day 3 — First follow-up (light bump)

Send only if no reply. Same thread, new message.

> Following up on this — totally fine if it's not your thing, just didn't want it to get lost in your DMs.
>
> If easier, I can send the questions over text instead of a call. Whatever's lower friction for you.

**Why this works:**
- Acknowledges DMs are chaos. Removes embarrassment of having missed it.
- Adds a second option (async text). Some coaches will say yes to that who wouldn't to a call.
- Doesn't repeat the original ask — they already saw it.

---

## Day 7 — Final follow-up (door-closer)

Send only if still no reply. Same thread.

> Last note from me — closing the loop on this. If timing's better in a few weeks, the door's open. Otherwise, no worries, keep crushing.

**Why this works:**
- Removes the awkward "are they going to keep messaging me forever" feeling.
- Re-opens the door without re-pitching.
- ~50% of replies in cold outreach come on the door-close. People feel guilty.

After Day 7, mark in DB as `Passed — no reply` and stop.

---

## They replied "yes / sounds interesting / what's the call about" — Booking reply

> Awesome, thanks {first name}.
>
> Here's my Calendly: {link}. 20 minutes, pick whatever works. Once it's booked I'll send the gift card same day so you have it before we talk.
>
> Three things I'll ask about, so you're not walking in cold:
> 1. How you currently program for clients (tools, templates, tweaks)
> 2. How clients pay you and how you handle drop-offs
> 3. The most annoying recurring part of running your coaching biz
>
> Talk soon.

**Why this works:**
- Sends the gift card *before* the call removes "is this a scam" friction. Cost of the gift card is way lower than a no-show.
- Telling them the questions in advance dramatically increases call quality. They show up with answers, not stalling.
- Three questions is the right number. Two feels like a survey, four feels like a job interview.

---

## They replied "what app are you building" — Disarm reply

This will happen. Have an answer ready.

> Honestly — I don't know yet. That's why I'm doing the calls. I have a hypothesis (something between FITR and a CRM, built specifically around how Hyrox programming actually works), but I'd rather hear what you'd actually use before building anything. Talking to ~20 coaches first, then deciding.

**Why this works:**
- Honesty disarms. "I don't know yet" is rare and credible.
- Shows you've thought about it (FITR + CRM framing) without committing to specs.
- "20 coaches first" signals seriousness — you're not a hobbyist, you're running a process.

---

## They replied "send me the questions instead" — Async path

> Totally — here you go. No rush:
>
> 1. What tool(s) do you use to program for clients today, and what do you wish they did differently?
> 2. How do clients pay you (FITR, Stripe, Venmo, in-person), and what % drop off in a typical month?
> 3. What's the most annoying recurring part of running your coaching biz that has nothing to do with actual coaching?
> 4. If a tool built specifically for Hyrox coaches existed, what would have to be true for you to switch?
>
> Whenever you have 5 minutes. Gift card's still on the table once you reply.

**Why this works:**
- Same three buckets as the call, plus a switching-question (Q4) you can't ask on a call without it feeling like a pitch.
- "Whenever you have 5 minutes" lowers the activation cost.
- Gift card stays gated on reply, not on send — reduces freebie-hunters.

---

## They replied "send me the gift card first" — Decline cleanly

> Fair — but I send the gift card after we get on the call, not before. Plenty of coaches I've talked to so far if it's not a fit, no hard feelings.

**Why this works:**
- Holds the line without being a jerk.
- "Plenty of coaches I've talked to" implies social proof + reduces their leverage.
- Most legit coaches will not push back here. The ones that do are the wrong sample anyway.

---

## After the call — Thank-you + gift card

Send within 2 hours of the call ending. Same day, every time.

> Really appreciate the time, {first name}. Your point about {specific thing they said — e.g. "coaches getting stuck rebuilding the same templates every week"} is going straight into the design doc.
>
> Gift card incoming — {Starbucks/Dunkin'/local coffee shop} $25, sent to {email/phone}. Let me know if it doesn't show up.
>
> If/when this ships, you'll be first to see it. And if any other Hyrox coaches come to mind who'd be worth talking to, an intro would mean a lot.

**Why this works:**
- Specific callback proves you listened. This is the single most underrated thing in research outreach.
- Confirms gift card delivery channel so they don't worry it got lost.
- Soft ask for referrals — at peak goodwill is when this works.

---

## Notes on volume + pacing

- **Send no more than 15 cold DMs per day from one IG account.** Above that, IG starts shadowbanning. Spread across morning/afternoon.
- **Never send the exact same text twice in a 48-hour window.** Vary the opener reference (the post you're calling out). The middle paragraph can stay constant.
- **Track everything in the Notion DB.** Every send, every reply, every no-reply. The DB is the asset, not the messages.
- **Reply window:** if they reply, get back within 2 hours during waking hours, max same-day. Cold DM warmth decays fast.
- **South Florida first, then expand.** In-person meeting offer is a real differentiator there. Mention it explicitly to FL coaches: "If you're in South FL I can also just buy you the coffee in person — I'm in Fort Lauderdale."

---

## Variant: South Florida in-person opener (Day 1 only)

Use this for any coach within ~50 miles of Fort Lauderdale.

> Hey {first name} — saw your {specific reference}. Really good.
>
> I'm in Fort Lauderdale and building something for Hyrox coaches — currently doing research, not selling anything. Trying to figure out how coaches who actually do this for a living run their day-to-day.
>
> If you're around South FL, I'd buy you a coffee for 20 minutes of your brain — your pick of spot. If remote's easier, happy to do a call instead and send a $25 coffee card.
>
> Either way, appreciate the work.

In-person conversion rate on this kind of outreach is meaningfully higher than remote. Use it whenever the geography fits.

---

## What "good" looks like after one week

- 50 coaches in the DB, qualified
- 25 Day 1 DMs sent (5/day × 5 days)
- 8-12 replies (32-48% reply rate is normal for this kind of specific, low-ask outreach; below 20% means the opener needs work)
- 4-6 calls booked
- 2-3 calls completed by end of week 2

If reply rate is under 20% after the first 25 sends, the problem is almost always (a) the specific reference is generic, or (b) the coach isn't actually qualified. Re-tighten qualification before changing the script.
